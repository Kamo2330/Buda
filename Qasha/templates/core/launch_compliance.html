Before you launch in South Africa

This page is a practical checklist, not legal advice. Have a South African attorney review your setup before you take real money or market publicly.

Copyright & third-party assets: This site uses Bootstrap (MIT licence), Font Awesome (free licence — keep attribution), and Google Fonts (see their licence). Do not copy text, photos, or logos from other rental sites. Use your own listing photos and original copy.

Business & registrations

  • Register a company or sole proprietorship with CIPC if trading formally.
  • Register for tax (SARS) and understand VAT if turnover requires it.
  • Open a business bank account for platform income and payouts.
  • Clarify whether you need an FSP licence or payment intermediary rules if you hold client money (get legal advice).

POPIA (personal information)

  • Publish a clear Privacy Policy (what you collect, why, rights, contact).
  • Appoint an Information Officer and register with the Information Regulator if required.
  • Get consent for marketing; honour opt-outs; report data breaches as POPIA requires.
  • Use contracts with any hosting/email providers that process user data.

Consumer law (CPA) & marketing

  • Fair, clear listing descriptions — no misleading rent or fees.
  • Direct marketing (email/SMS/WhatsApp campaigns) must follow CPA and POPIA; registry/opt-out rules are tightening (check current NCC guidance).
  • Terms must explain you are a platform, not the rental contract party.

Rentals & property sector

  • If you or users act as property practitioners, PPCC/EAAB rules may apply — a pure tech marketplace model is different; confirm with a lawyer.
  • Hosts remain responsible for lawful rentals, deposits, evictions, and municipal bylaws.
  • Consider fair-housing / non-discrimination commitments in your terms.

Payments (before going live)

  • Replace demo card flows with a licensed gateway (e.g. PayFast, Peach, Stripe where available).
  • PCI-DSS compliant handling — never store raw CVV on your servers.
  • Clear refund/chargeback policy in Terms.

What Qasha already has in the app

  • Single user registration (no separate tenant/owner signup).
  • Terms & Privacy pages (drafts — need lawyer review).
  • Booking notifications to tenants via Messages when hosts accept/decline.
  • In-app messaging; POPIA-oriented signup consent checkbox.
